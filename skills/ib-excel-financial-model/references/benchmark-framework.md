# Benchmark Framework

## Evidence Hierarchy

Use benchmark support in this order:

1. Filed historicals
2. Official guidance
3. Consensus
4. Direct peers
5. Industry datasets or channel checks
6. Analyst judgment

## What Needs Benchmark Support

At minimum, support these lines:

- core revenue drivers for each segment
- gross margin or COGS logic
- SG&A ratio
- R&D ratio when material
- tax rate
- diluted shares when EPS matters

## Benchmark Row Standard

Each benchmark row should answer:

- What metric is being assumed?
- What external range or anchor supports it?
- What is the exact base-case assumption?
- Why is the chosen value above, within, or below the benchmark range?
- What date is the evidence from?

## Typical Sources

- annual and quarterly reports
- investor presentation
- earnings call transcript
- broker consensus snapshot
- peer filings
- trade association or regulator data
- app / channel intelligence when relevant

## Writing the Rationale

Keep the rationale short and decision-useful:

- "Set 2026E ARPU growth at 6%, near the low end of the 6-9% peer range because management is prioritizing retention over price."
- "Set gross margin at 34.5%, above FY2025A but below the best peer because mix improves while utilization remains below peak."

## Red Flags

- assumption has no source or peer anchor
- benchmark date is stale versus the forecast horizon
- base case sits outside the peer range without explicit reason
- revenue and margin assumptions imply mutually inconsistent operating conditions
