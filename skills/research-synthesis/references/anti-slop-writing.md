# Anti-Slop Writing

Use this file as the final pass after the analysis is already sound.

## Keep

- direct statements of what the evidence says
- distinctions between fact, inference, and uncertainty
- concise transitions only when they carry logic
- concrete nouns, numbers, dates, actors, and constraints

## Remove

- filler summaries that add no new information
- generic scene-setting paragraphs
- bullet lists that exist only to look organized
- abstract buzzwords standing in for specifics
- symmetric sentence patterns repeated for rhythm alone

## Rewrite Habits

Prefer:

- `The evidence suggests X because A and B.`
- `This conclusion is limited by Y.`
- `Source 1 and Source 2 disagree on Z.`

Avoid defaulting to:

- inflated abstraction
- consultant-style slogan lines
- overuse of `not X but Y`, `in essence`, `worth noting`, or similar stock transitions
- repetitive recap sentences at the end of every section

## Final Check

Before returning the answer, ask:

1. Does each section add new information?
2. Does each important claim have visible support?
3. Did I keep unresolved conflicts visible?
4. Can any paragraph be cut without losing substance?
5. Does this read like synthesis rather than polished paraphrase?
