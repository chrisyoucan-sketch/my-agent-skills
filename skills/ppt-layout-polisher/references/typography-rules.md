# Typography Rules

## Font Strategy

Use stable fonts with broad Windows availability.
Prefer one Chinese font family and one Latin font family, or a single superfamily when available.
Follow the fallback chains in [font-fallback-rules.md](font-fallback-rules.md).

## Hierarchy

Maintain visible separation between:

- slide title
- subtitle or section label
- body text
- annotation or footnote

Do not let adjacent hierarchy levels differ only slightly.

## Recommended Sizes

These ranges assume a standard 16:9 presentation page:

- title: `24` to `34 pt`
- subtitle or section kicker: `14` to `20 pt`
- body: `14` to `20 pt`
- annotation or source note: `10` to `12 pt`

Treat `14 pt` as the minimum default body size.
Do not go below `12 pt` body text unless the user explicitly accepts dense slides.

## Line Length

- Prefer about 18 to 40 Chinese characters per line for body text blocks.
- Avoid very wide text boxes that create long reading sweeps.
- For bilingual content, keep line lengths even shorter.

## Paragraph Rules

- Prefer bullets over large prose blocks.
- Keep line spacing around `1.15` to `1.35` for body copy.
- Keep paragraphs short enough that the viewer can scan the slide quickly.
- Use emphasis sparingly. Too much bold text removes hierarchy.
